# What Makes Us Unique

We believe by focusing on three areas we can build a service that is truly unique and able to meet the needs of the people we care for like no one else: -

# People Who Work With us

  • Our staff should be happy, we will ensure our staff enjoy coming to work
  • Our staff should be well trained, we will ensure they receive all the training and support they need
  • Our staff should be passionate, we want to work with people who believe in our mission

# People Who We Support

  • The people we support should be involved at every step, we will ensure they are involved in all of the decisions about their lives
  • The people we support should be defined by their strengths, we will ensure we celebrate and promote their strengths
  • The people we support should achieve their goals, we will focus on outcomes and successes

# Systems

  • The systems that we use should be effective, we will use technology to empower our services to deliver on their goals
  • The systems that we use should be efficient, we will make sure they never get in the way of people doing their jobs
  • The systems that we use should be informative, we will use data to support decision making

# Our Vision and Values

We aspire to ensure everyone who uses our services will be better off than if they had used an alternative. We do not aim to directly compete with existing providers, but rather to offer services that are so compelling that they are the first choice and they achieve something that no one else can.

We have three core values that we believe in passionately, these guide us in everything we do:

  • Always treating people how we would want to be treated ourselves
  • Striving passionately to be the best and not afraid to challenge the norm
  • Focusing on the positive, finding silver linings and never giving up

# Dress Code

The following things are expected when working at Tideway Care: Always dress smartly and be clean and tidy for work to act as a positive role model to the people we support. You may be required to work in a variety of settings and wear the most appropriate clothing. For instance; if you are supporting someone in court a formal appearance may be required but if you need to support someone on a gardening work scheme you will need a change of gardening clothing.

We want our employees to:
(A) portray a professional image
(B) show respect for others
(C) adhere to health and safety regulations

Specific requirements include:

  • footwear must be appropriate and safe- e.g. no flip flops allowed
  • no short skirts or mini skirts
  • no low neckline/back/vest tops
  • no offensive slogans, pictures or logos printed on clothes
  • no offensive body studs or rings on display
  • no offensive tattoos on display

# Mobile Phones & Company Devices

  • All staff are issued with a company-owned device (usually a mobile phone) which has been specifically set up to provide secure access to company resources and data. You are expected to complete mandatory training on IT security and Data Protection and to abide by the company policies when using these devices

  • You should not use company-owned devices for any personal activities or anything that does not directly relate to your work

  • You should not conduct any Tideway Care business or attempt to access company data from your own devices

  • During work hours minimal use of your own personal mobile phone at work is permitted, for example important or urgent communications with your family for instance. However, we generally recommend you leave it in a safe location and retrieve it when you finish your shift to avoid any unnecessary distractions from your work

  • You are not permitted to share your personal details with the people you support; this includes personal mobile phone numbers, if you need to provide a contact number you should give your work number or the office phone number

# Social media

  • You must consider the potential risks involved in using social media and the impact that inappropriate use could have on you, your family, the people we support, their families and your colleagues

  • Our services require a high level of confidentiality and professionalism; it is not permitted for anyone to share any of their personal details with the people we support and your duty to protect the personal information of those people at all times

  • The use of social media to communicate with the people we support and their families is not permitted outside of an agreement with them signed by all parties for the benefit of the person. This agreement might be for the person to have set up a YouTube page to portray their personal journey and they might want staff to like their posts and send voice messages in support. This agreement is permissible if all staff are signed up to this but not if you are singled out to be the only person permitted to do this. This may impose on personal boundaries

  • The use of social media to blog, comment, post or tweet about Tideway Care success is permitted within the following boundaries. The material does:

    • Maintain the confidentiality of another staff member or person we support and keeps their personal information safe
    • Only names someone else with their explicit permission
    • Shows Tideway Care in a positive light
    • Shares good practice
    • States when the opinions are personal and not those of Tideway Care
    • Cyber bullying or indeed any form of bullying is not tolerated at Tideway Care

# Lone Working: Safety First

  • The Suzy Lamplugh Trust defines personal safety as ‘An individual’s ability to go about their everyday life free from the threat or fear of psychological, emotional or physical harm from others.’

  • In your work at Tideway Care you may work with people who have histories of having been violent or aggressive or possibly threatening in other ways, in relation to their behaviours that challenge. It is important that staff are skilled up to manage behaviours that challenge and fully comprehend the lone working policy and safeguards in place to provide support when it is require do reduce the risk of harm. It is vital that robust risk assessment and management practices become second nature and tacit for our staff in all situations.

# Professional boundaries

Working closely with vulnerable people means that you frequently get to know very intimate and personal details about them; this makes it more difficult to draw boundaries around your relationship with them as you develop very close and trusting relationships.

It is for this reason we have frequent supervision with our staff to discuss these sorts of issues should they arise. Always utilise your supervision to share concerns or issues emerging, reflect on them and consider learnings.

The following are some ways in which you must seek to keep your professional and personal boundaries with the people we support and their families:

  • Person centred - You are expected to place the needs of the person you are supporting at the centre of any decisions that you make about them and their lives. "It's not about you"!

  • Self-disclosure. Information about yourself and your personal life should not be disclosed to the people you support or their families.

  • Dual relationships. You should not hold more than one type of relationship with someone you support, for example, they cannot work for you, be a family member or receive extra private support from you, as well as be one of the people you support at Tideway Care.

# Sickness and sick pay

  • Staff are asked to report to their immediate line manager or nominated deputy as soon as it is clear they are not able to attend work
  • Staff member can self- certificate for up to 7 days
  • Staff members are required to send their line manager a completed doctor's sick certificate if the period of sickness is going to last more than 7 days.
  • Staff members are required to notify their line manager as soon as they are aware that they can return to work.
  • The manager will facilitate a return to work interview
  • If you are too ill to work you are entitled to statutory sick pay (SSP) for up to 28 weeks paid by Tideway Care.
  • To qualify for SSP and have been off work sick for 4 or more days in a row (including non-working days).

# Pension

  • To help people save more for their retirement, all employers are now required by law to provide a workplace pension scheme for certain staff and pay money into it.
  • You are eligible if you meet all of the following criteria:
  • You earn over £192 per week (or £833 per month)
  • You are aged 22 or over and
  • You are under state pension age.
  • You can opt out of the pension scheme if you want to, but if you stay in you will have your own personal pension when you retire. Your pension will belong to you, even if you leave us in the future.
  • Both you and we will pay contributions into it every pay period, and the government will also contribute through tax relief.
  • You will also receive a starter pack of information from NEST once the scheme starts.

If you have any questions about pensions please contact accounts@tidewaycare.com

# Raising concerns

We want you to always feel able to raise any concerns you might have. There are many ways to raise a concern and you should always feel safe to do so in whatever way suits you.

If you wish to raise a concern you can do so by;

# Speaking to a Manager

You can raise any concerns by speaking directly to your line manager or any member of the Tideway Care management team.

Your regular supervision meetings with your line manager should be a great opportunity to do this but you can also contact any member of the SMT directly and feel confident that your concerns will be taken seriously and acted upon.

# Contact Tell Tideway

If you don't know who to speak to about your concern you can contact Tell Tideway.

Tell Tideway is our public-facing feedback channel and all feedback received here is treated equally. Any concerns raised through this channel will be taken seriously and followed up by the SMT.

# Whistleblowing

If you wish to raise a concern anonymously you can complete the Whistleblowing form on the staff SharePoint. This form does not identify who filled it out in any way unless you choose to add your name to the form (e.g. if you wish to receive a direct response to your concern).

# Staff Grievances

  • Our staff should be able to come to work without fear of bullying, harassment or unfair treatment.

  • Reporting: If someone is making you feel uncomfortable, unfairly treated, intimidated or is behaving in an offensive manner towards you, please report any concerns to your line manager.

  • If your concern is either about your manager or you are unable / do not want to tell them for some other reason, please feel free to speak to any member of the senior management team.

  • Anything you say will be treated in strict confidence and your concerns will not be shared with anyone without consulting you first

# Staff Support

At Tideway Care we strive to ensure our staff feel supported, we expect our managers to be accessible and able to respond effectively to any concerns or issues raised.

Examples of support that is available to you include;

  • Training – we believe training is vital to equip staff to be able to do their jobs well and expect staff to let us know if they require additional coaching support in an area or indeed identify any new training needs
  • Supervision – we seek to provide at least monthly supervision and categorise supervision in the following types
    • Management Supervision
    • Practice Supervision
    • Debrief
    • Ad hoc meetings
  • Monthly Workshops

# Appraisal: ADORE

Annual appraisals will be taken after your 6 months' probation period has past and you have had a period of consolidation in your job role. We will expect to undertake your first appraisal before the end of a year.

Annual Review: reviewing last year’s OKRs how did we do?

Development Needs Reflection: Reflecting on whether we met the development needs

Objectives and Key Results Setting: Agreeing the annual OKRs

Reaching for the Stars: Looking at stretching ourselves and going the extra mile

Engagement: Agreeing how we work together to achieve the results we want
