# OP05: Appendix 6 - Co-Production and Person-Centred Planning

Appended to:

# OP05 - Programme Development

The Care Act defines co-production in the following way:

“Co-production is when you as an individual influence the support and services you receive, or when groups of people get together to influence the way that services are designed, commissioned and delivered”

The TLAP National Co-production Advisory Group says the following about co-production:

“Co-production is not just a word, it is not just a concept, it is a meeting of minds coming together to find shared solutions. In practice, co-production involves people who use services being consulted, included and working together from the start to the end of any project that affects them. When co-production works best, people who are supported by services and carers are valued by organisations as equal partners, can share power and have influence over decisions made”

At Tideway Care, we want coproduction to be at the core of how we develop people’s programmes together with them , their families and other significant people in their lives. We facilitate rather than dictate and we coach rather then tell.

The six principles of co-production are:

  • Recognising people as assets
  • Building on people's capabilities
  • Developing two-way, reciprocal relationships
  • Encouraging peer support
  • Blurring boundaries between delivering and receiving services
  • Facilitating rather than delivering

Since Valuing People first talked about Person Centred Planning (PCP) in services for people with a learning disability, the terms has started to lose its core purpose. Person Centred Planning is a process of constant review, learning and listening. PCP focuses on the immediate and the future, taking into account the needs, thoughts, concerns and opinions of the individual, and consulting their family and friends and others within their ‘personal network’ or ‘circle of support’. It is not merely about saying you are putting the individual needs at the heart of what you do. It is easy to say but can be a challenge to achieve.

The person centred approach helps individuals identify their aspirations, and mobilises those concerned - including their personal network, service staff, non-specialist and non-service sources - to help people pursue their own personal ambitions. Services should, in theory, become more flexible and tailored to the needs of the person, rather than a group of people with differing requirements. All too often the wishes of staff can take precedence over those of the people they support.

At Tideway Care we strongly advocate for people to have the opportunity to identify their goals and dreams and be able to participate in planning how they achieve them. This is checked at frequent reviews and staff are encouraged to create visual prompts and schedules to remind them of what has been agreed and to help them keep track of their own progress.

There are many Person-Centred Planning (PCP) tools available to assist us in getting to know the people we support, highlighting the strengths and assets, helping them establish where they want to get to and what a great life looks like. We have resources and training in place to support our staff to utilise these tools. Examples are:

  • One Page Profile:

    • When a person enters our service, we want to get to know them and therefore completing the one-page profile is a good way to get to know a bit about them.
    • If they do not have good communication skills you can ask family members to help or get them to pick out pictures that mean something to them.

  • Circles of Support:

    • We seek to develop circles of support with the people we support; ensuring they lead this process as far as is possible. Circles of support are a network people who are willing to support someone to achieve their goals.

  • Path:

    • The path process can take many hours, even days to formulate depending on how easily the person we support can engage in the process. It may be that the development of the Path documentation /audio / video will assist with defining someone’s objectives, key result areas and goals.

  • Health passports:

    • For people with high levels of healthcare needs or for those who don’t have high levels of need but want to access healthcare without feeling anxious of distressed; we may complete or ask the LD NHS Team to complete health passports which detail:
      • The areas of health need
      • How the person wants to be involved in their healthcare
      • Medication
      • Communication aids
      • Preferences
      • Etc

Both Coproduction and Person Centred Planning seek to involve and keep the people we support at the forefront of their care and provide more opportunity for them to truly have choice and control in their lives.
