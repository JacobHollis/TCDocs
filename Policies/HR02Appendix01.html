# HR02: Appendix 1 - Values Based Recruitment - Example Questions

Appended to:

# HR02 - Recruitment

A value-based process may involve offering a scenario in which you request the candidate chooses out of provided options.

e.g. Adam has autism and becomes very anxious when he meets new staff for the first time. As a new member of staff, you are asked to help Adam get dressed this morning and you are worried this will upset him because he has not met you before. What do you do?

Option Possible Outcome Score If Chosen
a) Go to Adam’s room to assist him without question It is good you wish to be helpful but Adam may get anxious and upset 1/5
b) Refuse to go as you have not met him before This is the most unhelpful option and suggests you do not want to help or learn how to best approach Adam 0/5
c) Ask the staff member to come with you to help as you have not met him before This may help and the staff member can introduce you to Adam and explain to him who you are. If he gets upset you can leave and the other staff member can continue to support him. If he is okay with you the other staff member can leave you to finish supporting him 5/5
d) Ask the staff member for guidance on how to approach Adam and then go alone This shows a willingness to learn and to get it right for Adam. It may however leave you exposed and also leave Adam without support for a time if you have to leave because he is upset 4/5
e) Ask the staff member to go this time and suggest you spend time with Adam later when he has had his breakfast as this is a time he is more relaxed. Then offer to do it next time This may be the better option if Adam is already agitated this morning. It shows a willingness to learn and do the right thing for Adam. It may however, leave the staff member upset because they have to do the support and you cannot do the task they were going to do 3/5

Here are some other values based questions you may like to use:

# 1. Tell me about what your greatest success was and why?

Value Tests

  • Check if they can recognise their own abilities and if they thrive on success
  • Check if they know how to reflect on successes and recognise contributing factors
  • Check if success is important to them
  • Consider the reasons they think this success is their greatest

Scoring: 1 point for each area you think they cover well and fit with our values.

# 2. What are you passionate about?

Value Tests

This question can either receive a one statement responses relating to something personal and not work related or can open up a whole series of responses and validation of why they are passionate about these things.

Ask them about what they are passionate about in work if they do not cover this at first.

Scoring Rate on a scale of 1 (poor response) - 5 (great response)

# 3. Sometimes our staff work long hours to help cover shifts if someone calls in sick at the last minute. How would you deal with this?

Value Tests

This tests our teamwork and flexibility. Also about whether they are aware of working time directives and what impact this may have on delivering quality support.

Scoring Rate on a scale of 1 (poor response) - 5 (great response)

# 4. What excites you about working with people with a learning disability and or autism?

Encourage further discussion with these additional questions:

  • What have you done to find out about working in the care sector?
  • What challenges do you think there would be?
  • What rewards do you think there would be?
  • What parts do you think you would most enjoy?

Value Tests

Checks their attitude to working with people with a learning disability and working on the care sector.

Checks why they are applying for the post and reasons

Scoring: 1 point for each area they cover positively in their response

# 5. Give me an example of a time when something in your work changed, such as your hours or what you were required to do in your job.

Encourage further discussion with these additional questions:

  • How did you respond to the changes? What did you do?
  • What was the outcome?
  • How do you feel about change? Checks out flexibility and team work.

Value Tests

Considers their approach to change

Scoring: 1 point for each area they cover positively in their response.

# 6. Can you give an example of where you’ve spoken up because you had concerns?

Encourage further discussion with these additional questions:

  • How did it feel to you?
  • What were the outcomes?
  • If it was successful – why do you think it was successful?
  • If it was not successful – why do you think this was the case, and what would you do differently?
  • What did you learn from this situation about yourself?

Value Tests

  • Checks their approach to safeguarding and raising concerns.
  • Checks their ability to reflect and consider their attitudes and approaches.

Scoring Rate on a scale of 1 - 5 where:
1 = poor response – little self awareness / poor awareness of need to raise concerns
5 = great response – great self awareness. Shows knowledge of keeping people safe from harm and how to reflect on incidents
