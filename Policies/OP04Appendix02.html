# OP04: Appendix 2 - Guidance on Initial Fact Finding for Complaints

Appended to:

# OP04 - Complaints

Once a verbal complaint is received, the person receiving the complaint must ensure that they get as much information as possible about the complaint to assist them in assessing the seriousness and/or the complexity of the complaint.

The following process is a guide:

  1. Listen carefully to the issues being raised by the person

  2. Identify if there are multiple issues relevant to the complaint and separate each issue. Attempt to identify any hidden or underlying issues that may exist.

  3. Summarise the issues to clarify and check that you understand what the person is telling you.

  4. Ask them to confirm that they agree with your interpretation of their complaint.

  5. Find out from them what they want to happen as a result of their complaint.

  6. Summarise the issues to clarify and check that you understand what the person is telling you.

  7. Thank the person for taking the time to make the complaint

  8. Empathise and acknowledge the feelings of the complainant.

  9. Explain to the complainant that there will be no negative repercussions

  10. Explain what will happen next e.g. you may need to contact your manager

Assess the verbal complaint. Staff should only attempt to manage complaints received at the point of contact if due care has been taken to establish that all issues can be addressed appropriately at the point of contact.

Check what actions are to be taken as a result of the complaint with your line manager.
