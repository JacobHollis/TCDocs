# OP05: Appendix 1 - Risk Assessment and Management Procedures

Appended to:

# OP05 - Programme Development

# 1. Introduction

The term ‘Risk Assessment’ can cover so many areas and can be confusing in terms of being clear about what sort of risk assessment is required for a given scenario. Most people we support in Tideway Care services will have had a risk assessment for their clinical mental health, forensic (history of criminal offences) , physical health , road safety, exploitation, and more.

It is therefore important to understand what has been previously assessed in the longer-term past and the recent past. We know that given the right environment and support, risks can reduce and we also know that people can be very unpredictable. Our model of Positive Behaviour Support and Intervention means we have a close monitoring of identified behaviours that challenge, physical and mental health needs and any concerns that have been raised.

We strongly believe that the more the people we support engage and lead their own support goals the more risks are reduced. The following guidelines assist our staff teams to understand the different risk assessments needed in a given scenario.

# 2. Risk Screening

Risk screening is completed within the Tideway Care Programme Manager as part of the initial assessment of someone referred for support. The form provides prompts for staff to consider any risks that may require management plan to be integrated into behaviour support and support plans.

The form is divided into 11 risk areas;

Risk Area Notes
Risk from Others e.g. sexual vulnerability, financial vulnerability, social vulnerability
Risk to Self e.g. suicide, self-harm, harmful/hazardous substance misuse, Pica, self-inflicted cuts, ties ligatures, overdose of medication, self-injurious, head banging, biting
Risk to Others e.g. aggression, violence, arson, sexually abusive behaviours, associated criminality
Risk of Neglect e.g. health, personal, degree of substance misuse, mental health deterioration
Risk to Children e.g. physical, emotional, sexual
Risk from Physical Complications e.g. medical, sensory, nutrition, hydration, choking, seizure activity, swallowing, diabetes
Risk of Going Missing e.g. tendency to wander and gets lost, goes off travelling without telling anyone or runs off in the community when staff escort
Risk of deterioration of Mental Health e.g. changes in daily living skills, increased anxiety, decreased motivation, withdrawal, unexplained changes in behaviour
Risk of Substance Misuse e.g. alcohol, illicit drugs, recreational drugs, legal highs. Etc
Risk when bathing/showering e.g. seizures, over/under sensitivity to heat of water
Other Risks e.g. road safety awareness, cultural, racial, sexuality, etc

To complete the risk screen staff can indicate 'Yes' to any of the risk areas that are relevant and complete details of the risks in the text box provided for each relevant areas. Where risks are identified in a risk screen the text should include details of mitigations that are or should be put in place.

# 4. Mental Capacity Assessments

a) Anyone can undertake a mental capacity assessment and determine if someone lacks capacity in an area or more than one area where they are unable to make informed choices and stay safe.

b) Only Social Services can determine if someone needs to have a Deprivation of Liberty Safeguard (DoLS), this safeguard is to protect people who lack capacity and require restrictions to keep them safe where they are now protected under the Mental Health Act.

c) A Best Interest Decision can be made by a professional in consultation with significant others to enable staff to act in the best interests of a person where they may disagree or refuse a certain approach / treatment / support plan action.

# 5. Functional Behavioural Assessment

a) This will be carried out by a qualified Behaviour Practitioner and will be a comprehensive look at the meaning of behaviours exhibited, their triggers and the environments where people respond better.

b) The assessment will detail each behaviour in their own context and any associated links between certain behaviour. This helps understand the context of the behaviour and gives indicators of what will help to reduce them.

# 6. Social Vulnerability Assessment

a) There is no validated assessment tool for social vulnerability in terms of vulnerable adults so the toll has been developed by Tideway Care to assist both the people we support and the staff to consider contributory factors to increasing social vulnerability and potential for abuse.

b) Factors that increase social vulnerability are:
   i. Disability
   ii. Loneliness
   iii. Lack of understanding about money, drugs, alcohol, sex, etc.
   iv. Lack of confidence
   v. Not knowing how to get help and support
   vi. Not knowing what is lawful

c) Remember: safeguarding is everybody’s business; if you see it or think it, report it to your line manager immediately.

# 7. Mental Health Assessment

a) There are two assessments; one which can be done with the people we support on any day where the staff think there may be evidence of a change in mental health. The other is a longer look at MH symptoms and signs.

b) Remember to note in care notes any changes in risk and what you have done about it.

c) Please refer to guidance : Mental Health Risk Assessment and Management Procedures
