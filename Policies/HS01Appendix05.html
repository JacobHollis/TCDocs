# HS01: Appendix 5 - Road Safety

Appended to:

# HS01 - Health & Safety

# 1. Safe driver:

  • Tideway Care expect that staff are competent and capable to drive on Tideway Care business especially when driving with people we support in a company or their own car.

  • We will check the validity of driving licences and insurance on recruitment and periodically afterwards MOTs?

  • We expect all staff driving on Tideway care business who drive the people we support to have read these Road Safety guidelines and comply with their requirements.

  • We expect them to also carry out routine safety checks, such as those on lights, tyres and wheel fixings, and report any faults?

  • They must also be clear about their plans for getting assistance if their vehicle breaks down, e.g. use safety warning triangles and high-visibility jackets, have a Tideway Care team member to come and assist with the people we support if they are in the vehicle.

  • Staff are expected to drive within the law and this includes not being under the influence of drugs or alcohol when driving, not using a hand held mobile phone, not speeding, etc.

  • If a staff member uses their motorcycle of bicycle to travel on Tideway Care Business they are expected to wear a crash helmet and protective clothing for motorcycle and bicycle riders are of the appropriate standard?

# 2. Staff are expected to check the following prior to taking someone in their car:

  • Whether the vehicle is fit for the purpose for which it is used

  • Whether seatbelts and head restraints are fitted correctly and that they function properly

  • Whether drivers have access to information that will help them reduce risks (without breaching patient confidentiality in the case of using Taxi firms)

  • Whether it is safe to transport people we support or equipment in the type of vehicle

  • A comprehensive risk assessment must be conducted in each case, the outcome of which will be the development of safe systems of practice to meet the needs of the people we support concerned and advice should be sought from the Team Manager.

  • Whether their vehicle is registered, has a valid MOT (if older than 3 years old) is taxed, has business insurance and that the lights, horn, mirrors, brakes, etc are operating correctly, i.e. their vehicle is fit for use on the public highway

  • That passengers wear seatbelts at all times and that when used to carry equipment, their vehicle is correctly loaded and that the loads are secure

# 3. All drivers are expected to maintain and drive their vehicles in a safe manner, for example, drivers shall:

  • Ensure the vehicle is driven in accordance with the Law

  • Ensure their vehicle is roadworthy

  • Ensure the vehicle is not overloaded

  • Never carry passengers other than in the seats provided

  • Never drive for excessive periods; (e.g. for longer than a period of 4.5 hours as defined by the Vehicle & Operator Services Agency guidance ‘Rules on Drivers’ Hours & Tachographs, 2007 ) and ensure that regular breaks are taken. U

# 5. Update their managers immediately on any matters concerning their eligibility to transport passengers in private vehicles; such as (for example)

  • Any incurred driving violations/suspensions in licence o if the vehicle is considered no longer roadworthy

  • If insurance cover or MOT expires

  • If the staff member is claiming expenses for the trip it will be necessary to comply with the Expenses Policy.

  • Drivers must also inform their manager if they have committed any driving offences.
