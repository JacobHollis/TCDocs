# HS01: Appendix 4 - Prevention of Falls Procedure

Appended to:

# HS01 - Health & Safety

  1. Falls Risk of people we support: in most cases the people we support will have a falls risk assessment and management plan in place if they have a long term medical condition (e.g. epilepsy, diabetes, hypotension, hypertension, tinnitus etc) or physical disability that impacts their mobility. Other reasons they may be vulnerable to falls would be:
  • Taking medication that causes dizziness or unsteadiness
  • Frequently under the influence of alcohol or drugs
  • Recovering from illness and unsteady on their feel
  • Visual impairment
  • a diagnosis of osteoporosis or osteopenia
  • a recent hip replacement
  • cognitive impairment or dementia/delirium
  • significantly impaired mobility
  • recent history of recurrent falls
  • a history of falls or fear of falling

  1. Clinical Falls Risk assessment: Falls risk assessments should be carried out by a qualified professional when determining if someone is at risk of falls. There are several risk assessment tools used, e.g. the Morse Falls Scale, (Falls Risk Assessment Tool-FRAT, etc. If you think someone is vulnerable to falls, please support them to be referred for a full assessment if they have not had one.

  2. Where there are identified risks ensure there is a support plan that details a falls prevention plan. Consider whether assisted aids and assistive technology can help.

  3. There are many simple ways to control slips and trips risks and prevent accidents and injury in the home. Here are a few examples.

  • Stop floors becoming contaminated
  • Use entrance matting
  • Get any leaks fixed
  • Make sure that your cleaning method is effective for the type of floor you have
  • Remove spillages promptly
  • Check for loose, damaged and worn flooring and get replace as needed.
  • Make sure lighting is sufficient and that slopes or steps are clearly visible.
  • Keep walkways and work areas clear of obstructions.
  • Have the right footwear
