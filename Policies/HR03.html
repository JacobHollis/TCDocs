# HR03 - Expenses

This document forms part of Tideway Care’s policy Framework and should be read in accordance with the following other policies:

Policy Reference Policy Name
G04 Anti-Bribery

# Scope and Application of this Policy

The policy applies to all employed staff working in all services provided by Tideway Care; specifically, this is anyone with a contract of employment with Tideway Care.

# Legal & Regulatory Context

This policy has been produced with reference to the following legislation, guidance and directives;

  • HMRC Guidance – Expenses and employee benefits

# Equality Statement

In the implementation of this policy, Tideway Care will not discriminate against any colleague or customer on grounds of their race, ethnic origin, gender, sexuality, marital status, disability, age, religion or class.

# Policy Statement

Tideway Care will reimburse employees for all legitimate business expenses incurred while delivering Tideway Care’s services in line with guidance and limits set.

# Policy Outcome

After reading this policy you will be able to:

  • After reading this policy you will understand what business expenses are covered by the company and how to claim for their reimbursement.

# 1. Introduction

1.1 Tideway Care will reimburse employees for reasonable expenses wholly, necessarily and exclusively incurred in connection with its business. Employees should not be either financially disadvantaged or advantaged because of genuine business expenses.

1.2 Employees are responsible for the payment of all expenses they incur, except where we have direct settlement arrangements with suppliers in place.

1.3 Breach of this policy is a disciplinary offence. Deliberate falsification of a claim or the evidence needed to make a claim will constitute gross misconduct. This may result in summary dismissal. "Falsification" includes the failure to pass on any discount obtained in the course of incurring an expense.

# 2. Approval and reimbursement

2.1 Tideway Care will reimburse the reasonable costs of amounts necessarily expended in the performance of the duties of the employment, for example to travel to events, external meetings, training etc.

2.2 This does not include travel between home and the primary place of work unless the employee is recognised as being a "contractual home-based employee". The employees primary place of work is either the services central office or the location of the employee’s first shift of the day.

2.3 Employees should travel by the most cost-effective mode of transport taking into account journey time and the nature of the journey as well as the monetary cost.

2.4 Expenses should be submitted in line with the Expenses Guidance procedure document attached to this policy

2.5 Expenses will only be reimbursed if they are:

  • Supported by detailed receipts
  • Submitted on the company’s expenses claims system which is fully and correctly completed and authorised
  • Submitted for authorisation within one month of the end of the month of being incurred
  • Claimed in line with this policy

2.6 Authorised expenses will be paid directly into the employees bank/building society account. Unless otherwise requested this will be the same account used for the employee’s payroll.

# 3. By employee's own car/motorcycle

3.1 Employees may claim for use of their own vehicle if used in connection with Tideway Care business. These claims must be in line section 2.2 of this policy.

3.2 The mileage allowance is set by reference to the HM Revenue and Customs (HMRC) rules on AMAPs (Approved Mileage Allowance Payments). These rates are located on the HMRC website.

3.3 The employee is required to keep track of business mileage incurred in the tax year (6 April to 5 April) to ensure the correct rates are used.

3.4 If an employee carries any other employees in their own car or van on business travel, that employee can claim 5p per passenger per business mile. The passengers must be employees and they must also be travelling on business journeys.

3.5 Where an employee uses their own vehicle, the employee should ensure that the vehicle is in good working order, fully insured (cover must include business travel), taxed and has a valid MOT if required.

Fines for traffic violations (e.g. speeding, parking) will not be reimbursed by the company. They are the responsibility of the driver.

# 4. Public Transport

4.1 By train: Employees should, as far as possible, travel standard class and should book their journeys sufficiently in advance to obtain the best possible prices.

4.2 By plane: Flights should, whenever possible, be booked sufficiently in advance to obtain the best possible prices. Air travel should be by economy class. Employees should retain all tickets and credit card vouchers.

4.3 By taxi: Employees are expected to use taxis only when strictly necessary and where it is cost effective to do so. Employees should always obtain a receipt.

# 5. Late night travel

5.1 In exceptional circumstances, and only in accordance with HMRC rules, Tideway Care may meet the cost of late night taxis or similar road transport provided all the following circumstances apply:

  • the employee is required to work later than usual and at least until 9.00pm;
  • this occurs irregularly; and
  • by the time the employee ceases work either public transport has ceased or it would not be reasonable to expect the employee to use public transport.

5.2 The number of such journeys must not exceed 60 in any tax year.

# 6. Hotel accommodation

6.1 Should be booked in advance at the best possible rates which should not exceed £130 per night (£150 within the M25), including VAT but excluding breakfast costs. Any booking in excess of that amount will need to be approved by the operations director prior to booking.

6.2 Employees should take into account the location of the hotel as regards the cost of taxis, etc and the time required to travel to and from the hotel.

# 7. Staying with friends or relatives

7.1 An employee may claim an allowance of up to £35 per night. Receipts are not required but the employee must confirm that the overnight stay was necessary in connection with travelling on Tideway Care business.

7.2 An employee claiming this allowance cannot also claim reimbursement of actual accommodation and subsistence costs although they can claim personal incidental expenses (see below).

# 8. Subsistence – overnight absence – UK

8.1 Tideway Care will reimburse reasonable out of pocket expenses incurred by the employee when it is necessary to stay away from home overnight on Tideway Care business. This will include the following:

  • hotel bills (see section 10)
  • breakfast
  • lunch, evening meal and beverages - to an overall maximum of £40 per day (please note that the employee may incur costs in excess of £40 but may only claim up to £40).
  • parking at hotel

8.2 Additionally, the employee may claim "personal incidental expenses" up to a maximum of £5 per day. This covers items such as telephone calls, newspapers and laundry.

If the employee is away for more than one night, the daily limits (ie £40 and £5) may be averaged across the number of nights that the employee is away.

# 9. Subsistence – no overnight absence – UK

9.1 An employee may claim for meals and beverages up to a maximum of £10 per day when travelling on Tideway Care business. This is intended to reimburse the employee for additional expense as a result of having to travel on Tideway Care business.

# 10. Entertaining – business

10.1 Employees should only entertain visitors and guests where it is likely to assist Tideway Care in its corporate objectives.

10.2 In general, entertainment counts as business-related if its purpose is to discuss a particular Tideway Care service, maintain an existing Tideway care connection or to form a new Tideway Care connection.

10.3 By contrast, entertainment of Tideway Care acquaintances won't count as business-related if its purpose is social - even if there's some discussion of business-related topics in the course of the entertainment.

# 11. Entertaining – staff

11.1 The cost of entertaining other Tideway Care staff is it at the discretion of the operations director and must be approved in advance of the expense.

# 12. Eye tests and spectacles

12.1 Tideway Care recognises its obligations under the Display Screen Equipment (DSE) Regulations 1992 (Amended 2002). The Ombudsman Service will contribute to the cost of an eye test if you use video display unit (computer monitor) for a significant part of your working day.

12.2 Tideway Care will contribute £25 towards the cost of an eye test conducted by a suitably qualified optician. If the test reveals that spectacles are required for exclusively video display unit (VDU) work, Tideway Care will contribute £75 towards the cost of basic spectacles. This policy does not apply to contact lenses.

12.3 Tideway Care will only reimburse one eye test in any 12-month period.

12.4 Tideway Care will not contribute towards the cost of spectacles with any element of everyday use, in other words the spectacles must be exclusively for VDU use.

# 13. Professional subscriptions

13.1 Employees may claim back the cost of the annual subscription for up to a maximum of two professional bodies, provided that each body is on the HMRC approved list and its activities are directly relevant to the employee's duties. Further subscriptions may be claimed as a tax deduction on the employee's tax assessment return, provided they are also on the HMRC approved list
