# OP09: Appendix 5 - Scenarios

Appended to:

# OP09 - OP07 - Mental Capacity Act Healthcare

# Scenario 1

A is a 24 year old male with a learning disability; he wants to spend time with someone you suspect is taking money off him. What do you do? You think he lacks capacity and needs a power of attorney or appointee.

Action

  • Speak to A about his friend and find out more about their relationship.

  • Speak to A about his money and what he wants to spend it on or save for.

  • Speak to A about what he does when people ask him for money.

  • Speak to A’s family / significant others about whether they believe there are concerns and what action they think should be taken.

  • It is deemed that A has capacity and is able to communicate understanding of the issues and ability to make a choice even if others think it is a poor choice.

  • It is deemed A does not have capacity.

  • Talk to the team about your concerns and the discussions you have.

  • Complete an MCA assessment form and include social services.

  • If all agree

# Scenario 2

B is a 19 year old female who has recently transferred to a residential home from hospital and has a diagnosis of Aspergers and Borderline Personality Disorder. She has started cutting herself in the shower with her razor. What do you do? You think you should keep her razor and supervise her showering.

Action

  • Speak to B about her concerns about settling into her new home and whether she has specific issues with times of the day, showering, etc.

  • Ascertain if there is anything that will cause her to reduce the cutting or increase it.

  • Speak to her previous care team and ask if they had experienced this behaviour previously and note details.

  • Speak to her family and ask them if they know of the behaviour and risks and note details and any concerns.

  • Clarify if she has mental capacity.

  • B says she is unable to sleep if she does not cut herself in the shower. The cuts are superficial and she keeps them clean. She agrees to work on reducing this behaviour and work on some relaxation strategies instead. She is deemed to have capacity.

  • This may change and will need monitoring.

  • B says she is compelled to cut herself and she is unable to stop. Her anxiety is such that she is sometimes cutting deeper than superficial cuts and bleeding. She refuses to talk about working on reducing these behaviours. The hospital team and family tell you she has done this before and are concerned.

  • You hold a MCA assessment and Best Interest decision meeting with all involved.
