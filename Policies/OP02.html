# OP02 - Risk Management

This document forms part of Tideway Care’s policy Framework and should be read in accordance with the following other policies:

G01 Governance, Quality & Performance
HS01 Health and Safety
OP03 Incidents
OP03.1 Serious Incidents

# Scope and Application of this Policy

The policy applies to all employed staff working in all services provided by Tideway Care; specifically, this is anyone with a contract of employment with Tideway Care.

This is achieved by:

  • setting out respective responsibilities for operational, corporate and strategic risk management for staff and the Senior Management Team.
  • explaining the methods used to assess, monitor and mitigate both operational and corporate risks effectively.
  • delivering a clear policy framework to deliver the Tideway Care’s Risk Management Strategy.

This document defines the standards and procedures applicable to all Tideway Care staff for the effective management of organisational risks. The assessment and management of individual risks relating to the people we support is covered in the policies OP05 and OP06.

# Legal & Regulatory Context

This policy has been produced with reference to the following legislation, guidance and directives;

  • Health and Social Care Act 2008 (Regulated Activities)

# Equality Statement

In the implementation of this policy, Tideway Care will not discriminate against any colleague or customer on grounds of their race, ethnic origin, gender, sexuality, marital status, disability, age, religion or class.

# Policy Statement

This document sets out how all staff should approach the management of operational and corporate risks within their Team, Service, or the wider organisation.

# Policy Outcome

After reading this policy you will be able to:

  • Understand how to recognise the risks around you and how they relate to the organisation
  • Understand how to report identified risks and what to do if you have concerns
  • Be able to apply risk assessment and management best practice for delivering safe services

# 1. Introduction

1.1 Tideway Care is committed to actively managing operational and organisational risks through the implementation of a clear and robust risk management framework. We do this by being mindful of risks and proactively seeking to mitigate them.

1.2 All Care Providers are required by the Health and Social Care Act 2008 (Regulated Activities) Regulations 2014 to:

  • assess the risks to the health and safety of service users receiving care or treatment;
  • doing all that is reasonably practicable to mitigate any such risks

1.3 Tideway Care has a responsibility to assess, monitor and mitigate operational, corporate and strategic risks as part of ensuring the ongoing provision of safe and effective services.

1.4 Tideway Care acknowledges that it is not possible to eliminate all risks and we will encourage positive risk-taking where risks may result in positive benefits for the people we support, staff and visitors, where those risks can be properly assessed, understood and recorded.

# 2. Staff Responsibilities

2.1 All Tideway Care staff are, directly or indirectly, responsible for the safety of the people we support and will:

  • work within the principles and practice of all Tideway Care policies and procedures which are applicable to their area of work as this will, directly or indirectly enhance the safety of patients, colleagues and the public
  • be vigilant for potential risks and take responsibility for reporting or, where appropriate, completing a risk assessment
  • ensure completion of documentation as required within standard timeframes

2.2 All staff must report incidents and near misses in accordance with Tideway Care policy OP03 - Incidents; be aware they have a legal duty to take reasonable care of their own safety and the safety of others who may be affected.

# 3. Manager Responsibilities

3.1 Managing operational risk within their services and ensuring adherence to this policy and related procedures. Supporting all staff in their services are fully aware of their responsibilities towards risk assessment and management and proactively manage risk to improve the safety of services.

3.2 Escalating to the Senior Management Team where appropriate, any significant risks to the safe and efficient delivery of Tideway Care services, including operational, financial and reputational risks. Managing and monitoring these risks on a Risk Register to provide a complete overview of all potential risks to which a service is exposed; escalating to the Senior Management Team where appropriate.

3.3 Ensuring that staff in their services feel empowered to take action to identify, assess and mitigate risks.

# 4. SMT Responsibilities

4.1 Reviewing the risk register quarterly and considering risks escalated, for adding to the Corporate Risk Register.

4.2 Maintaining action plans to address identified risks where necessary and reporting to the Governance & Quality committee on the progress of those plans.

4.3 Ensuring resource is allocated within their services to assess and manage their risks.

# 5. Recording and Managing Risks on a Risk Register

5.1 All risks should be mitigated immediately upon their identification, where this is not possible they should be recorded on the risk register where they can be monitored, and action plans can be put in place.

5.2 The risk register should;

  • contain a summary overview of all potential risks to which a Tideway Care may be exposed;
  • evaluate the level of existing internal control in place to manage the risk;
  • be an ‘active’ document to record and report risks using the Risk Management process. This means it is regularly reviewed and updated

# 6. Monitoring Compliance and Effectiveness

6.1 The Governance and Quality Committee has overall responsibility for monitoring the effectiveness of this policy.

6.2 The SMT will monitor the service and Corporate Risk Registers and ensure all mitigating factors are in place and effective.

6.3 Monitoring will include quarterly planned service audits to highlight any areas for development and gaps in delivering mitigating actions.
