# Scope and Application of this Policy

The policy applies to all employed staff working in all services provided by Tideway Care; specifically, this is anyone with a contract of employment with Tideway Care. It also applies to contracted staff.

All staff have a legal duty to ensure their own safety at work and the safety of others, including colleagues and the people we support. Incidents should be reported when they occur in Tideway Care premises and outside of these premises whilst on Tideway Care business. Where an incident is categorised as serious, it should also be referred to the OP03.1 Serious Incidents Policy.

# Legal & Regulatory Context

This policy has been produced with reference to the following legislation, guidance and directives;

  • Care Act (2014)
  • National Patient Safety Agency (2009) Being Open Framework
  • Department of Health (2014) Statutory Duty of Candour for Health & Adult Social Care Providers
  • Duty of Candour 2015 Guidance
  • RIDDOR - Reporting of Injuries, Diseases and Dangerous Occurrences Regulations 2013 – Guidelines

# Equality Statement

In the implementation of this policy, Tideway Care will not discriminate against any colleague or customer on grounds of their race, ethnic origin, gender, sexuality, marital status, disability, age, religion or class.

# Policy Statement

This policy outlines the approach to incident reporting, management and investigation. It defines the types of incidents that may occur and describes the way in which we grade incidents. The policy provides a definition of an incident or near miss, and direction in reporting, and management of all incidents.

# Policy Outcome

After reading this policy you will be able to:

  • Understand what an incident is and how Tideway care defines incident categories and gradings
  • How to report, manage and undertake a proportionate investigation into incidents
  • How to appropriately gather information gathering and record on the Tideway Incident systems

# 1. Introduction

1.1 The health and safety of our staff and the people we support are a priority and we take our responsibilities in this very seriously.

1.2 An incident is an event or an occurrence that could have, or did result, in unnecessary damage, loss or harm. At Tideway Care we have a responsibility to report such incidents. Our strategy is always to prevent harmful incidents, however; should they arise we will respond quickly and efficiently to seek improvements and mitigate against reoccurrence.

1.3 The reporting and monitoring of incidents of actual or potential harm assists us to identify organisational, governance and service developments through rigorous review and investigation. We utilise lessons learnt as a mechanism for systemic advancements in our operations.

# 2. Roles and Responsibilities

# 2.1 ALL Staff

(including temporary, agency, bank, students and contractors) – are responsible and accountable for:

  • Identifying reportable incidents and raising concerns at the earliest opportunity to the relevant people

  • Ensuring the immediate safety of the people we support, and or other persons involved in line with this policy

  • Reporting the incident to their line manager and others as determined by Tideway Care Policy and Guidance and escalating concerns should responses not fully address the incident response required

  • Undertaking proportionate and appropriate immediate and follow-up actions to reduce the potential for an incident to escalate or deteriorate and increase the risk or actual harm

  • Ensuring they complete all necessary documentation to report, record and evidence the circumstances of the incident and the immediate actions undertaken

  • Participating in incident review and agreed implementation of service improvements as a result of lessons learnt

# 2.2 Managers of Teams or Services

are responsible and accountable for:

  • Ensuring this policy and associate procedures are implemented throughout our services and their teams. Recording and monitoring any exceptions or breaches

  • Ensuring staff are supported to carry out their duties in respect to this policy

  • Demonstrating that all staff (including temporary staff) have received appropriate training in these arrangements

  • Reviewing the content of incident reports regularly and responding to and escalating any concerns as required

  • Completing the investigation and recording accurately what has been done, by whom, the outcome, recording the learning to prevent reoccurrence and how this is to be shared with all involved including the individual to whom the incident pertains

  • Ensuring appropriate corrective actions are implemented following an incident, escalating concerns where management is outside of their area of control or where the incident or its impact may affect others

  • Providing staff, the people we support and or other persons involved in the incident with appropriate professional or personal support or access to such support as appropriate; including a debrief, information about next steps and support to implement improvements

  • To report incidents which are covered by Regulation 18 of the Health and Social Care Act (2008) directly to the Care Quality Commission

# 2.3 Operations Director

in addition to the above, is responsible and accountable for:

  • Promoting the importance of this policy to all staff, including temporary staff

  • Developing and maintaining a robust governance assurance and performance framework which ensures:

    • All incidents are reported and investigated in accordance with this policy and supporting procedures.
    • All necessary and agreed actions required are fully implemented, and evidenced; and lessons learned are shared
    • Incident trends are monitored, identifying any trends and outlying events - using this information to support the identification of risks; and taking additional scrutiny and action as required, addressing any concerns

  • Ensuring compliance with the NHS Serious Incident Framework

2.4 The Managing Director has overall accountability for Health & Safety and Risk Management. The Senior Management Team is responsible for promoting an effective and transparent culture within the Tideway Care

2.5 Senior Managers will need to familiarise themselves with the different obligations for reporting to government authorities and understand their duties as an employing organisation and care provider

# 3. What is an Incident

3.1 The World Health Organisation defines reportable incidents as: ‘Incident’ – an event or circumstance that could have, or did result, in unnecessary damage, loss or harm such as physical or mental injury to a patient, staff, visitors or members of the public.

3.2 An incident is something that has occurred; in the context of this policy we want people to report any incident that has the potential to cause harm or harm or damage has happened. A serious incident is where the harm is graded as medium to high and it is more permanent and has significant impact. We have detailed some examples of incident grading and serious incidents in Appendix 1 – Categorisation of Serious Incidents.

3.3 A Near Miss is an incident that had the potential to cause harm but did not at this time. This might be wrong medication being offered someone and the person we support tells you rightly it is the wrong tablet. The tablet is not given but it might have been harmful if the person being supported hadn’t noticed the error and it had been given.

3.4 Each incident will need to be categorised and the level of reporting required identified at the earliest opportunity. As a rule of thumb, always report to your line manager or supervisor as soon as possible within 24 hours of the incident occurring or having been brought to your attention.

3.5 If in any doubt but you have a ‘gut feeling’ talk to your line manager or supervisor as soon as possible.

# 4. Initial Actions

4.1 Safety is the first priority for any incident or near misses – ensure all people involved are out of harm's way.

4.2 Support for people involved following the incident to be provided immediately after the incident but also some time later when the impact and emotional responses can emerge after the initial shock. Put a plan in place to ensure support is available in response to the incident as required.

4.3 Document the detail of the incident as required by the Tideway guidance- as a minimum complete the incident details on the Tideway Care Incident system.

# 5. Follow Up Actions

5.1 Review incident with the team to identify any further actions required and report any concerns to relevant people. See section 7.

5.2 Talk to the person/s we support if incident impacts on them and talk to their family if people we support agrees or if family are designated as carers and part of the team. Discuss with them any changes to their support plan that may be required ad update as agreed.

5.3 The shift leader will facilitate any debrief sessions required and will talk to the staff involved if any and discuss any lessons learnt and support / development needs identified.

5.4 The Programme Coach or Team Manager in their absence will activate any staff HR processes as required and review all medium to serious incidents in the team meeting.

5.5 The shift leader should collect any witness statements at the earliest opportunity whilst it is fresh in people’s minds; however their safety and wellbeing is the first priority.

# 6. Documentation

6.1 The person raising the concern/s must complete OP03-F01 24 Hours Incident Reporting Form and send to Team Manager to complete within 24 hours of the incident

6.2 If the incident is determined to be serious (see Appendix 1) you should refer to the serious incidents policy.

6.3 Staff should complete incident reports carefully with only necessary detail, stating the facts and avoiding speculation.

# 7. When and how to Notify Other People About Incidents

7.1 All incidents must be reported to the shift leader and logged on the Tideway Care Incident System within 12 hours of the incident occurring or being brought to your attention. Any member of staff will be able to log an incident following completion of the Tideway Care training session on incident reporting.

7.2 In addition; any incident graded as medium or serious must be reported verbally to the Programme and Rapport Coach (PRC) for the relevant person we support if they are involved. The Team Manager must also be informed immediately. If the Team Manager is absent the incident must be reported to the Operations Director instead

7.3 The Team Manager will report medium to serious incidents to the Operations Director who will inform the SMT. If the Operations Director is absent the Team Manager must report directly to the Managing Director

7.4 If the incident is also a safeguarding matter you must refer to the safeguarding policy and procedure

7.5 If the incident is categorised as a serious incident the Programme Coach or Team Manager must also report it verbally by phone to the following within 24 hours and initiate a serious incident review:
          a) Community care coordinator
          b) Nominated family member
          c) Commissioner

If they cannot be reached on the phone, leave a message and send a follow up email immediately with summary details. If unsure, refer to the Operations Director for guidance.

7.6 If there are OTHER organisations that need to know about the incident; e.g. CQC, RIDDOR or HSE the Operations Director will notify them.

7.7 A flow chart to assist with decision making around communication of incidents is included in Appendix 2

# 8. Investigation

8.1 All reportable incidents require some form of review or investigation. In most non-serious incidents this will involve a brief review of the incident and its antecedents and consequences (see OP06 – Positive Behaviour Support). Serious incidents will need a more detailed investigation and should be referred to the Serious incident review policy (OP03.1)

8.2 Every non-serious incident should be reviewed by the Team Manager within one week of occurrence to check the details entered and confirm that it does not require a serious incident review or other relevant investigation. Most incidents that require more investigation or follow up will involve investigation processes covered elsewhere (for example OP01 – Safeguarding Adults and Children)

# 9. Learning from Incidents

9.1 The purpose of reporting and investigating incidents is to ensure that the services learn and prevent similar incidents from occurring in the future.

9.2 Incident data should be used as part of Team Meetings, Practice Supervision, Management Supervision and reflective practice in order to learn from these events and identify trends.

9.3 Tideway Care will also share with commissioner’s thematic reviews of incidents as appropriate to share lessons learnt and any development plans put in place as a result.

9.4 The Operations Director will also share with teams any outcomes of nationally published Serious Case Reviews and the applicable lessons learnt.
