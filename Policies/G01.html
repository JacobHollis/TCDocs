# G01 - Governance, Quality & Performance

This document forms part of Tideway Care’s policy Framework and should be read in accordance with all other Policies and Procedures.

# Scope and Application of this Policy

The policy applies to all employed staff working in all services provided by Tideway Care; specifically, this is anyone with a contract of employment with Tideway Care.

This policy supports the organisation, its employees and the people it supports to deliver the best outcomes at the highest standards it can possibly achieve.

# Legal & Regulatory Context

This policy has been produced with reference to the following legislation, guidance and directives;

  • Equality Act (2010)
  • The Care Act (2014)
  • The Human Rights Act (1998)
  • Health Act (1999)
  • Mental Capacity Act (2005)
  • Safeguarding Vulnerable Groups Act (2006)
  • Children Act (2004)
  • Prevent Strategy (2011)
  • DOH Positive and Proactive Care: reducing the need for restrictive interventions (2014)

# Equality Statement

In the implementation of this policy, Tideway Care will not discriminate against any colleague or customer on grounds of their race, ethnic origin, gender, sexuality, marital status, disability, age, religion or class.

# Policy Statement

Tideway Care is committed to delivering safe & caring services of the highest possible quality. We do this by hiring talented and passionate people and putting systems and processes in place that support them to deliver a great service.

As an organisation we are keen to provide quality assurance, governance structures and constantly measure the performance of our service and use the information gathered to celebrate our achievements and make improvements. This document sets out the things we seek to measure and how we assure ourselves that we are always meeting and exceeding the expectations of the people we care for, our commissioners, our regulators and ourselves.

Using the CQCs Key Lines of Enquiry we have built a structure that seeks to answer 5 questions;
How we know the service(s) are safe
How we know the service(s) are caring
How we know the service(s) are responsive
How we know the service(s) are effective
How we know the service(s) are well-led

# Policy Outcome

After reading this policy you will be able to:

  • Learn about Tideway Care’s Vision and Values which set the culture of the organisation
  • Understand the importance of our Governance Framework and the policies and procedures it supports
  • Establish how the CQC Key Lies of Enquiry check the safety, quality and performance of our services

# 1. Governance: Tideway Care Vision and Values

KLOE:

Well Lead

1.1 We provide ground breaking services that support people to thrive and participate as equals in their community. We do this by helping people to turn the tide that has often left them feeling shipwrecked and abandoned. We work with them to move forward and find a way back to shore.

1.2 The core values that our company is built on:

  • Always treating people how we would want to be treated ourselves
  • Striving passionately to be the best and not afraid to challenge the norm
  • Focusing on the positive, finding silver linings and never giving up

1.3 We recognise that there will be challenging times for the people we care for and our staff; we will always seek to reframe the situation so that we learn, think about how we can change things to get a better outcome and consider all options. We will not do this in isolation, we will always strive to be inclusive and transparent in everything we do.

# 2. Governance: Leadership

KLOE:

Well Lead

2.1 Organisational Structure:

2.2 Tideway Care is committed to implementing quality and governance processes and structures to improve service delivery and the experience of the people we support.

2.3 The governance and quality committee has the following features:

  • The Operations Director is appointed as the lead for the governance and quality of our services and chairs the Governance and Quality Committee

  • There is a clear line of accountability from the Senior Management Team (SMT) to the services. The SMT consists of Tideway Partners and the Operations Director of Tideway Care

  • The committee has consultation with and representation from the people we support and family carers as far as is possible. We also consult with local Experts by Experience who act as a critical friend and advocates for the people we support and their families.

  • Governance, Quality and Performance is a standing item on the Senior Management Team Meetings

  • Tideway Care is committed to the management and direction of the quality and risk management programme and has in place the assurances to ensure its implementation

2.4 The following are standing agenda items:

  • Feedback - This includes hot topics for members of the committee / satisfaction survey feedback /service feedback fora / complaints / compliments / etc

  • Care Governance - This includes safeguarding / outcome monitoring / incidents /dignity and respect / coproduction and involvement / participation / etc

  • Key Performance Indicators - Looks at quality Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) achievements / barriers across services

  • Leadership performance - Objectives and Key Results (OKR) achievements / barriers to achieving OKRs

  • Human Resources governance - Sickness / absence / appraisal completions / supervision exceptions / equality monitoring / etc

  • Health and Safety - Any issues related to health and safety including security of premises, fire safety compliance / RIDDOR / etc

  • Information governance - Confidentiality breaches / record management system issues /etc

  • Finances - Any issues relating to the people we support money management / cost pressure concerns / etc

  • Risk Register - Review risk register and amend as appropriate

  • Audit and Research - Share any audit / research undertaken since last meeting / follow up any action plans

# 3. Governance: Human Resources - Management

KLOE:

Well Lead

3.1 Tideway Care is committed to the management and direction of staff and has the structures and committees in place to lead this. We have policies in place that specifically promote equality of opportunity, e.g. in terms of gender, race, religion and disability, Ref: G02 - Equality, Diversity & Inclusion.

3.2 There is an active recruitment policy and we ensure vacant posts are filled quickly with well qualified candidates. Staff sickness rates, vacancies and turnover are monitored and acted upon when necessary. We have a staff retention and wellbeing scheme in place to support our staff well. Ref: HR02 - Recruitment

3.3 Staff Wellbeing - The wellbeing of our staff and good staff morale is recognised as of high importance and we have robust staff feedback programme in place including a forum in which staff can discuss morale issues with senior management. Ref: HR01 - Employment

3.4 We have in place structures to support our staff to develop and attain new skills through:

  • Appraisals
  • Learning, Development and Training
  • Induction and Foundation Training
  • Supervision

3.5 There are clear procedures for managing complaints from staff, accessing occupational health services, grievance procedures, sickness management, good and poor performance management, pastoral staff support, etc. Ref: OP04 - Complaints and Ref: HR01 - Employment

3.6 Workforce planning is linked to service planning and the skill mix of teams is reviewed at least annually.

# 4. Governance: Human Resources - Training & Supervision

KLOE:

Safe

4.1 Tideway Care believes the health and safety of our staff and the people we support are of utmost importance and have put in place the governance to check we are maintaining the highest of standards in health and safety.

4.2 There are sufficient numbers of skilled staff to safely meet the needs of people we support at all times. Tideway Care complies with relevant health and safety legislation in relation to:

  • Risk assessment and management
  • Personal Safety which includes reference to reducing violence affecting staff
  • European directives on working time

4.3 Practice Support: In addition to time with line managers the following are ways in which the organisation seeks to provide support to staff.

  • Practice supervision: This is to focus on staff interactions with the people we support and their families to explore ways in working with them to improve outcomes. It is undertaken with a PBS Practitioner and will occur weekly.
  • Reflective practice: Reflective practice is about taking time either individually or in a group to think and review what has occurred and consider whether things could have been different if the staff interactions or the environment had been different. This is useful after an incident or a complaint has bene made or to work through a scenario that has not gone well or one that has gone very well to clarify lessons learnt.
  • Informal supervision: This is when staff seek some advice or want to check something out with a peer or line manager. It is not documented or recorded in anyway but provides you with support. Sometimes this can be just before leaving a shift and they want to talk about an incident that has occurred before they leave.

4.4 Induction training is provided for temporary, locum and permanent staff before they have unsupervised access to people we support.

4.5 Basic skills and mandatory training requirements for support staff are met through our foundation course for new employed staff, these include:

# 5. Governance: Information Governance

KLOE:

Safe & Well Lead

5.1 People have the right to make decisions about how and when data about them is used by companies. The regulations on data protection make it clear that data about a person should be treated like that person’s property. In principle this means they should have some agency in how it is used, and companies should not use it in ways that the person would not reasonably expect.

5.2 We have in place a policy which ensures that personal data is used in accordance with these principles and the responsibilities placed on staff to properly consider people’s rights before taking any action. Ref: G05 - Data Protection.

5.3 Staff have access to training and support in using Tideway Care’s Electronic and paper-based systems, confidentiality, data protection, access to and use of information. The safe storage of personal details are maintained in accordance to the General Data Protection Regulation 2018. Ref: G06 - IT Security.

5.4 There are key guidelines in place to alert staff to permissible internet use and materials permitted to be shared on social media.

# 6. Governance: Health and Safety

KLOE:

Safe & Well Lead

6.1 We have a responsibility to maintain the people we support’s independence, privacy and personal choice whilst maintaining a safe living environment and a safe working environment for staff. We will seek to provide guidance and help to identify hazards and reduce risks in a reasonable and proportionate manner.

6.2 Tideway Care has systems for assuring Health and Safety in the workplace and our policies and procedures have been written in line with the advice of the Health and Safety executive (HSE) guidance. cover the management of fire, infection control, legionella, lone working, etc.

6.3 Health and Safety indicators have been developed and are monitored and used to improve the quality of information. Health and Safety audits are undertaken, staff receive feedback on the outcomes and quality of the information they provide.

# 7. Governance: Equality and Diversity

KLOE:

Well Lead & Responsive

7.1 Tideway Care operates within the general duties of the Single Equality Act (2010)

7.2 Tideway Care places a commitment to Equality, Diversity and Inclusion across all operations and our services and we have the mechanisms in place to ensure an inclusive working environment where differences are valued and equality of opportunity is advanced.

# 8. Governance: Quality Improvement & Audit

KLOE:

Effective & Responsive

8.1 Tideway Care is committed to the management and direction of the quality and audit programme and plans and monitors audits at the governance and quality committee. Staff, people we support and their families help identify audit topics and agree standards.

8.2 There are mandatory health and safety audits undertaken for all services and a CQC themed audit for all registered services is completed on an annual basis as a minimum.

8.3 Health and safety audits will include:

  • Keeping safe at home: Ref: Keeping Safe at Home Easy Read Leaflet and Check list; Security in the home, Kitchen Safety, Bathroom Safety
  • Lone working
  • Safe storage of medication
  • General Data Protection compliance
  • Quality of support documentation

8.4 All support programme audits will be carried out with the consent and engagement of the people we support; encouraging them to help with the review and think about how to reduce any risks identified. The individual’s Programme Coach will lead the audit if the people we support are unable to.

8.5 All other Governance, safety and quality audits will be led by the Team Manager and or Operations Director with the involvement of the staff teams.

8.6 Audit findings and recommendations are reported to Tideway Care board, quality and teams, the quality and governance committee and other interested parties. Audit findings are used to inform service planning

# 9. Governance: Complaints

KLOE:

Responsive

9.1 Tideway Care has processes for effectively dealing with complaints and monitors and reports on complaints at the Quality and Governance Committee and the Senior Management Team meetings. Complaints are monitored and used to inform service development, e.g. records of complaints are referred to in management meetings.

9.2 Staff are made aware of complaints that are relevant to their work and the outcome of the complaints process.

9.3 The people we support, their families and relevant stakeholders are aware of the complaints management process and the complaints reporting procedure is clearly advertised. Ref: OP04 - Complaints.

# 10. Governance: Incident Reporting

KLOE:

Safe & Responsive

10.1 Tideway Care has up-to-date procedures for the reporting and management of serious incidents and near misses. Guidance and protocols have been developed in consultation with staff for dealing with specific incidents and identified risks and detail staff requirements to report risks and adverse events. Ref: OP03.1 - Serious Incidents and OP03 - Incidents.

10.2 Tideway Care promotes an open, blame-free culture for reporting incidents created by our reflective practice processes which looks for lessons learnt rather than scapegoats.

10.3 Tideway Care has provided information to staff regarding anonymous reporting of adverse events and "near misses" (this information may also relate to "whistle-blowing" and reporting unsafe practice)

10.4 Incident reviews, investigation findings and recommendations are reported to Tideway Care SMT, the governance and quality committee, the staff, the people we support and their families and other interested parties. They identify trends and inform action plans and service planning.

# 11. Governance: Safeguarding

KLOE:

Safe & Responsive

11.1 There is monthly scrutiny of safeguarding incidents by the Senior Management Team.

11.2 The Managing Director holds the overall responsibility for safeguarding adults and children and will chair the Safeguarding Board Meetings.

11.3 The Operations Director will be the Safeguarding Lead and act as the central point of contact for all staff with regard to safeguarding matters and advice.

11.4 We work in partnership with local safeguarding teams in a transparent and open manner at all times and are clear about how we fully utilise their local safeguarding processes and procedures.

KLOE:

Caring & Effective

12.1 Tideway Care is committed to communicating with people we support and their families and understanding their needs and priorities.

12.2 Tideway Care encourages the people we support and their families in the planning and monitoring of services. These include:

  • Co-production
  • Person Centred Planning
  • Consultation

# 13. Quality: Rights to Privacy and Dignity

KLOE:

Caring & Responsive

13.1 Tideway Care ensures that in any mixed gender services that there is privacy and dignity between men and women. In supported living services where men and women live together; any identified privacy and dignity issues will be assessed and support plans put in place to reduce the risk of any offence or harm.

13.2 The environment of accommodation and facilities are safe, clean and comfortable and they have access to food and drink in relation to their dietary and hydration needs. In supported living accommodation we understand the balance between personal choice and maintain safe environments. We will always seek to coach the people we support in keeping safe, clean and comfortable in their homes.

13.3 If the people we support require personal care, their support plans will identify any personal preferences including how they like their dignity, privacy and respect maintained. REF: Personal Care Procedures

# 14. Quality: Access to Services

KLOE:

Caring & Responsive

14.1 Tideway Care has processes for effectively ensuring we respect Equality & Diversity and will monitor the implementation of individual support plans to ensure individual needs are met in respect of cultural, spiritual, disability and dietary needs. E.g. an interpreter is used when necessary who understands the sensitivity, diversity and complexity of our people we support’s needs.

14.2 Tideway Care has processes for effectively meeting the people we support’s Mental health and Physical Health Needs and ensuring access to any necessary medical equipment they require. We facilitate access to specialist healthcare and community services and ensure good nutrition and hydration for all the people we support. REF: OP05 - Programme Development and OP09 - Physical Healthcare

14.3 Tideway Care has processes for effectively meeting the people we support’s community engagement needs and actively foster partnerships with community services

# 15. Quality: Service Delivery

KLOE:

Caring & Effective

15.1 Tideway Care has the processes in place to ensure we deliver the legal requirements of the Care Act in respect of our policy and procedure for the support planning process and positive behavior support model.

15.2 Tideway Care assess the quality of service delivery through a number of metrics; we set outcomes with the people we support, their families and significant others such as care coordinators, networks of support, community services, etc. See OP05 - Programme Development.

15.3 Outcome Monitoring: One of the ways in which we evidence how well we deliver our services is through the monitoring of the things that are important to the people we support and the people responsible for funding that support. The way we measure success will be through a combination of personal goals achieved (qualitative) and data on a number of agreed metrics (quantitative).

15.4 Support Plan Goals: Each person will have their individual support plan goals that will be measurable and reviewed frequently. The Programme Coaches will work with the people we support to develop support plan goals and the Programme Facilitators will be responsible for implementing the actions required to support them to achieve their goals.

15.5 Our Senior Management Team monitors the outcomes measured for each person that we support on a quarterly basis at their SMT Meeting. We compile regular feedback loops to the people we support and their significant others as agreed with them on a regular basis.

15.6 We monitor staff supervision and appraisal goal setting and ensure action is taken to seek continuous professional and personal development to increase the efficacy of our services

# 16. Performance: Organisational Learning

KLOE:

Well Lead & Effective

16.1 Tideway Care has processes for effectively sharing, implementing and learning from Evidence Based Practice to promote continuous improvement and facilitate innovation and thought leadership. This is led by the Senior Management Team and informed by the reports from the Governance and Quality Committee.

16.2 Tideway Care has processes for effectively communicating with Commissioners and other relevant professionals. We keep them up to date with any changes in the presenting needs of the people we support and any serious incidents that occur. We also respond to feedback on our service delivery and contract compliance and implement development plans as a result of this feedback.

16.3 We monitor staff supervision and appraisal goal setting and ensure action is taken to seek continuous professional and personal development to increase the efficacy of our services. REF: HR01 - Employment.

# 17. Performance: Operational Responsibility

KLOE:

Well Lead & Effective

17.1 The Operations Director is the chair of the Quality and Governance Committee and reports directly to the Senior Management Team of Tideway Care. They hold responsibility for the operational delivery against all standards. The committee terms of reference include as its responsibilities the following:

  • To ensure coproduction and person-centred support is being delivered
  • To provide quality assurance, governance structures and constantly measure the performance of our service and use the information gathered to celebrate our achievements and make improvements
  • The Operations Director is appointed as the lead for the governance and quality of our services and chairs this Committee; they will ensure the meeting is documented.
  • There is a clear line of accountability from the Senior Management Team and the services
  • The committee has consultation with and representation from the people we support and family carers as far as is possible; we will facilitate pre meetings to support them if they wish.
  • Governance, Quality and Performance reports to the Senior Management Team Meetings and is responsible for flagging up any current and emerging concerns and issues.
  • Tideway Care is committed to the management and direction of the quality and risk management programme and this committee will seek assurances to ensure its implementation.
  • The committee agrees priority areas for improvement and development
  • The committee approves priority areas for audit / research
