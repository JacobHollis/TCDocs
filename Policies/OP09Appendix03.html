# OP09: Appendix 3 - Antipsychotic medication and physical health checks

Appended to:

# OP09 - Physical Healthcare

# Prescribed antipsychotic medication

The physical monitoring recommendations for the people with Serious Mental Illness and prescribed regular antipsychotics are as follows:

Criteria Baseline for all 4 Months after initiating or switching antipsychotics Annually
Personal & family History ✔️
Waist circumference ✔️ ✔️ ✔️
Weight ✔️ ✔️ ✔️
Height ✔️ ✔️ ✔️
Blood pressure ✔️ ✔️ ✔️

  • An ECG is desirable, or as soon as practically possible, on admission and 6 monthly if a the person is on antipsychotics or has pre-existing cardiac disease (see Physical Examination Record)

  • If the person is on antipsychotics and has pre-existing cardiac disease and/or other physical risk factors, ECGs should be undertaken a minimum of every 3 months or more often if clinically indicated

  • If the person is on high dose antipsychotics, an ECG should be taken every 3 months; if on combination antipsychotics an ECG should be performed minimum 6 monthly

  • Diabetes is associated with significant morbidity and premature mortality. A high prevalence of diabetes and impaired glucose tolerance is seen in people with schizophrenia and other severe mental illness

# Metabolic syndromes

Current Definition of Metabolic Syndrome

The current International Diabetes Federation definition of Metabolic Syndrome is as follows:

  • Central obesity

AND any two of the following:

  • Raised triglycerides - (>=1.7mmol/L) OR specific treatment for this lipid abnormality.
  • Reduced high-density lipoprotein cholesterol (<1.03mmol/L in males and <1.29mmol/L in females, OR specific treatment for this lipid abnormality.
  • Raised blood pressure (systolic BP >=130mmHg or diastolic85mmHg) OR treatment of previously diagnosed hypertension.
  • Raised fasting plasma glucose (>=5.6mmol/L) OR previously diagnosed Type 2 Diabetes.

Further ethnic specificity values available at https://www.idf.org

Staff should be aware and seek medical help if the people they support shows any medical distress and to alert any help provided to the fact they are on antipsychotic medication as appropriate.
